Aromatherapy

Aromatherapy is a form of massage that uses essential oils extracted from plants, known as "essential oils," to create a sensory experience that impacts both physical and mental well-being. Essential oils are often derived from flowers, fruits, leaves, or roots of various plants and have aromatic properties that can contribute to relaxation and mood enhancement. In aromatherapy massage, essential oils are typically combined with massage techniques applied to the body or specific areas that may be experiencing tension or discomfort, such as the back, shoulders, or neck. This practice aims to reduce stress, promote muscle relaxation, and enhance overall relaxation in the body. The essential oils used in aromatherapy have various health benefits, such as relieving stress, alleviating pain, stimulating blood circulation, or even promoting better sleep. It is essential to note that using aromatherapy should be done by individuals with knowledge of essential oils, and it's advisable to start with small amounts to prevent allergic reactions and allow the body to adjust to the aromas before using larger quantities.
